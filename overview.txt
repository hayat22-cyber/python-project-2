This project analyzes supermarket sales data to understand product performance, category-wise sales distribution, and customer purchasing patterns. The objective is to derive meaningful business insights using data cleaning and analysis techniques in Python



The dataset contains sales records with the following fields:

Date
Product
Category
Quantity
Sales
CustomerID

The data was cleaned by removing duplicates, standardizing column names, and converting date fields to proper datetime format.



Key Insights

 Category Performance

Electronics category generates the highest revenue.

Groceries have frequent sales but lower revenue per transaction.

 -Product Analysis

High-value items like Laptop and TV contribute significantly to total sales.

Low-priced grocery items sell in higher quantities.

 -Sales Trend

Sales are consistent across days with spikes driven by high-value electronics purchases.







Business Insights

Electronics products drive maximum revenue and should be prioritized.

Groceries ensure customer footfall and repeat visits.

Premium products have lower quantity but higher profit impact.











Recommendations
Increase stock of high-revenue electronics products.
Run combo offers on groceries to increase basket value.
Introduce discounts on electronics during peak days.
Use customer purchase history for targeted promotions.





Conclusion

The analysis provides actionable insights that can help improve inventory management, sales strategy, and customer targeting. Data-driven decision-making can significantly enhance supermarket profitability.
